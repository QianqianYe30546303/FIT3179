Residential Property Market in Greater Melbourne 2016-2018

An overview of facts & prospects based on real data collected from Domain.com

Number of properties for sale vs. actually sold by suburb

The left visualisation shows the number of for-sale properties per hundred in each suburb. This reflects the population and housing mobility in the corresponding suburb and whether it is a popular place for people to invest in real estates from 2016 to 2018 to some extent.

By contrast, as shown in the visualisation on the right hand side, the variation of the number of properties being successfully sold per hundred across Melbourne is presented with extreme values highlighed in yellow (minimal conversion rate) and darker red (higher conversion rate) to assist investment decision making.

It has shown that in general, different from the variation that an increasing proportion of properties were for sale as it approaches the metropolitan and the regioinal area has a smaller housing market and lower mobility, properties located in regional Melbourne were more likely to be sold.

Median Housing Price Growth in Greater Melbourne over Quarters

There had been an overwhelming downward trend in median housing price in Melbourne with far greater negative quarter-over-quarter growth rate from 2016 to 2018 comparing to positive growth.
Looking at the figure each year, only median housing price in 2016 has shown a positive growth rate. Both the year of 2017 and 2018 had an overall negative growth rate and appeared to continue to fall at a rapid pace. This is a warning signal for the potential house buyers to be cautious with their purchase decision making if they do consider this as an investment.

How is housing price affected by its attributes?

With respect to the location, there is a general trend that the house was sold at a higher price as it is closer to the Melbourne CBD. Most properties sold clustered in between 5 to 25 km away from the CBD.

Units are smaller in size and generally closer to the CBD at a lower price range under $0.8M. Unit price is also the least correlated with the distance. It usually depends on the size of the property (bedroom number).
House prices are very much related to its location. Plenty of them within 5km from the CBD were sold high even with a smaller size. For those located further than 3km from CBD, size is also an highly key factor that affects the price.
Townhouses usually have a room number of 2-3, mostly located within 18km from the CBD. Similar to houses, generally, it had a higher price if located close to CBD, however, can be easily purchased at a lower price range between $500K and $1M.
